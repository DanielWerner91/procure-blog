## Top Story

**[US 2026 National AI Policy Framework proposes sweeping federal procurement standards](https://procure.blog/articles/us-2026-national-ai-policy-framework-proposes-sweeping-federal-procurement-stand)**
The White House released a national AI policy framework that would preempt state-level AI rules and require all federal procurement to follow standardized unbiased-AI principles. Federal contractors face new compliance requirements that could reshape how AI vendors sell to government agencies.
*Policy | Apr 10*

## Enterprise & Research

**[GSA's New AI Clause Would Mandate U.S.-Made AI Systems in Procurement Contracts](https://procure.blog/articles/gsa-s-new-ai-clause-would-mandate-u-s-made-ai-systems-in-procurement-contracts)**
A proposed GSA clause would require all contractors and subcontractors to use American-made AI systems for federal work, with broad data rights and reporting obligations attached.
*Policy | Mar 30*

**[NIST to Address Standards for AI in Agentic Procurement](https://procure.blog/articles/nist-to-address-standards-for-ai-in-agentic-procurement)**
NIST announced plans for a public-private convening in April 2026 to set standards for AI agents used in commercial procurement, following a surge in autonomous purchasing tools.
*Policy | Mar 27*

**[New research urges procurement teams to redefine supplier risk management in 2026](https://procure.blog/articles/new-research-urges-procurement-teams-to-redefine-supplier-risk-management-in-202)**
Studies from Achilles and the World Economic Forum call for expanded AI-based supplier risk approaches to handle volatility, compliance gaps, and data quality issues.
*Supplier Management | Apr 8*
